# Company context
_Last updated: 2026-06_

> We build automations and small internal apps for service businesses that have outgrown spreadsheets.

## The company
- **Name:** FlowForge
- **What we do (one line):** Done-for-you automation and lightweight internal tools.
- **Website:** flowforge.example
- **Stage / size:** 6 people, bootstrapped, profitable.

## Offer
- **Service / product:** Automation sprints — for ops teams drowning in manual steps — fixed scope, 2 weeks.
- **Service / product:** Internal app builds — for teams whose spreadsheet has become load-bearing — fixed price.
- **What makes us genuinely different:** We hand over documented, owner-operable systems — no lock-in to us. Clients can maintain what we build.

## Customers
- **Ideal customer:** 10–60 person service businesses (agencies, clinics, logistics), an ops owner who feels the pain.
- **The problem we solve:** Repetitive manual work and data scattered across tools.
- **The outcome we deliver:** Hours back each week, fewer errors, one source of truth.
- **Example clients:** A 30-person recruiting agency (cut onboarding admin ~70%); a regional clinic group (automated intake).

## Personas
- **Decision-maker:** Founder / COO — goals: scale without more headcount — pains: things break when someone's on leave.
- **User / day-to-day:** Ops manager — goals: stop copy-pasting — pains: tools that need a developer to change.

## Positioning & messaging
- **Category we compete in:** Automation agencies / fractional ops.
- **Value proposition (one sentence):** Systems you own, built in weeks, not quarters.
- **Proof / credibility:** 40+ shipped projects, named case studies, fixed-price guarantee.
- **Often compared to:** Hiring a junior ops person; big consultancies; DIY no-code.

## Voice
- **Tone:** Practical, confident, no hype.
- **Words/phrases to use:** "own your systems", "in weeks", "documented".
- **Words/phrases to avoid:** "revolutionary", "AI-powered" as filler, "seamless".

## Team
- **Who does what:** 2 builders, 1 solutions lead, 1 PM, 2 part-time.

## Client-facing know-how
- **Questions clients ask most (with answers):**
  - *"Will we depend on you forever?"* → No — we document and train; you can run and change it.
  - *"How long?"* → Sprints are 2 weeks; app builds 3–6 weeks.
- **Common objections & how we handle them:** "We could build it ourselves" → you could, but the maintainable version is the hard part; that's what we sell.
- **Our process, first contact → delivery:** Discovery call → fixed scope + price → build with weekly check-ins → handover + docs + training.

## Anything else
Never overpromise timelines to win a deal. Under-promise, over-deliver, keep clients for years.
